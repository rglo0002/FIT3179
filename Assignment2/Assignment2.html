Satellites of Earth

The first artificial satellite, Sputnik 1, was launched by the Soviet Union in 1957, marking the beginning of the space age. Since then, a significant amount of satellites have been sent to space, some of which have since returned to Earth.

Today, thousands of satellites orbit our planet, playing crucial roles in communication, weather monitoring, navigation, and scientific research. The data represented in this visualization focuses on the currently orbiting satellites, providing insights into their distribution and functionality in our modern world.
